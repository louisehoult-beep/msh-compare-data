Who’s who — what we can name, and what you have to earn

The Stakeholder Mapper names 17 roles across its product templates. This map shows, for each one, whether a real named individual can be obtained from a public source — and if not, why not. Every verdict is traced to a primary source. Nothing here is estimated.

The finding that matters: there is an almost exact inverse relationship between how much a role influences a device decision and how findable that person is. The roles the mapper flags as critical — procurement category manager, IPC lead, MDSO, EBME — are precisely the ones no public register lists. The roles registers do list reliably — Caldicott Guardian, SIRO, Information Asset Owner — aren’t in the mapper at all, because they rarely decide a device purchase.

That isn’t a gap to apologise for. The name of the category manager for your product line is only obtainable through the relationship — it cannot be bought, scraped or FOI’d. That is exactly why it is worth something.

Procurement — national category lead

NHS Supply Chain category manager
Source: NHS Supply Chain category contacts (~70 individuals, direct email and mobile). Published so suppliers can make contact — the strongest lawful-basis position of any source found, because your purpose matches the publication purpose.

Timing: NHS Supply Chain states the engagement window itself — 9 to 15 months before the current arrangement expires. Pair with its procurement calendar (~80 contract launches over 18 months).

Limit: these are national leads, not the category manager inside a given trust. They complement trust relationships; they don’t replace them. Handle under the outreach evidence standard — not a bulk list.

Payer — trust board tier only

Chief Finance Officer / Finance Director
Source: ODS Senior Information Risk Owner register — ~11,363 rows, roughly 280–293 at trusts and ICBs. Free, Open Government Licence, bulk downloadable. Email fill rate 31%.

Limit — read this before relying on it: the mapper’s Payer is the divisional / finance manager who holds the budget. That person is not in any register. The board CFO is findable; the budget-holder you actually pitch to is not.

Also freely available — but not roles your mapper asks for: Caldicott Guardian (~364–378 at trusts/ICBs, 86% email fill), Information Asset Owners (215 at trusts, 100% email fill), plus CMO / Medical Director / Chief Nurse / CIO. Useful for Information Governance routes on digital products — which is the one product type where this tier genuinely decides.

Any role at a trust currently tendering

Named contact on the notice
Source: Find a Tender OCDS API (use the API — the HTML notice pages return 403 to scripted requests).

Three things that change the number: there is no job-title field anywhere in the OCDS schema, so you cannot tell a Head of Procurement from a junior buyer. The 69.7% headline fill rate is inflated by Wales at 93.2% — England alone is 42.5%. And ~4% of names are departmental (“Procurement Team”) rather than people.

Counter-intuitive: Procurement Act 2023 notices score lower (44.4% vs 76.4%), because “person” in UK law includes bodies corporate. Don’t assume the new Act helps.

Why partial coverage is fine: a trust that is tendering is a trust worth a call, and the tender is your reason to make it.

EBME / clinical engineering

EBME / medical physics / clinical engineering
Source: Register of Clinical Technologists — therct.org.uk (public, free) — 688 named Medical Engineering registrants, 1,055 across all scopes. AHCS register adds ~160 more.

Hard limit: name and town only — no employer, no job title. It establishes who exists, not who works where. IPEM’s terms prohibit systematic downloading.

Roughly 10–15% of acute trusts publish named clinical engineering staff on department pages, skewed to large teaching centres. Realistic route is manual research per target.

Why — the three closed doors

Registers stop at the exec tier

Caldicott Guardians and SIROs are named because information-governance statute requires those accountabilities to be visible. Nothing requires a trust to publish who buys its wound care.

FOI does not open it

ICO decision IC-340495-F0W1 (2025) is almost exactly this case: a supplier asked a trust for the contracts manager’s name and email. The ICO backed the trust — accepting the commercial motive as a legitimate interest, but failing it on necessity.

The practical trap: FOI-obtained data triggers an Article 14 duty to tell each person where you got their details. Your opening line becomes “I obtained your details via a Freedom of Information request to your employer.” A materially worse opener than almost any alternative.

The profession doesn’t publish its own

CIPS runs ~54,600 public profiles but cannot be searched by employer — free text does not reach bios, so “NHS” returns nothing. UK pool ~480; a 60-profile sample contained zero NHS trust staff. Board papers are a firm negative too: across six trusts and ~170 author lines, Head of Procurement 0, MDSO 0, EBME 0, Category Manager 0.

Why LinkedIn is out — the practical reason before the legal one. Automated profile views appear in the target’s “who viewed your profile” feed, attributed to whoever is logged in. A Hub tool that scraped NHS staff profiles would surface in those people’s notifications — attributed to your members. That is a reputational risk you would be shipping to your customers. LinkedIn also returns HTTP 999 to programmatic access and its terms prohibit automated collection.

Correction worth carrying: hcsa.org.uk is NOT the Health Care Supply Association — it is a Hampshire schools netball site. The real HCSA is nhsprocurement.org.uk. Don’t let the wrong domain reach a config or outreach list.

What this means for how you sell

A rep looking at a map showing two evidenced names, five findable with a lookup, and eight marked “you must earn this” has a genuinely useful account plan. They can see the shape of what they don’t know. That is materially better than a tool that returns eight roles and leaves them guessing which are real.

The gap is the product. Anything a competitor could buy from a list is not an advantage. The eight roles in the red tier are your reason to be in the building.
